HOURS AFTER MY mother took her last breath on earth…

After we opened the windows to let the spirits out…

After we lit incense stick after incense stick…

After we watched MMA fighters on TV in the basement…

And the smoke kept rising. The chain must not be broken.

SECTION TWO. A second movement enters—steadier, broader.

The lens shifts. We compare two states of the same place…

One emerges as the other recedes, a dialogue of growth and memory.

The garden is an argument with time. We take sides, then reconsider.

And in that slow crossfade, the city learns how to breathe again.
